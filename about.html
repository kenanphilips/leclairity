About Karlie

I received my Bachelors of Arts degree in Psychology at the University of Victoria and my Masters of Counselling Degree from City University of Seattle. During my Masters Program, I was an Intern at Citizens’ Counselling Centre under the supervision of registered clinical counsellor, Marilyn Murdock. While working at Citizens’ and other organizations, such as Vancouver Island Health Authority, Salvation Army, and Aids Vancouver Island, I supported clients with various difficulties ranging from grief and loss, eating disorders, depression, anxiety, relationship difficulties, and different forms of trauma. Supporting clients who had experienced trauma, such as sexualized violence, inspired me to focus my thesis project on social responses towards sexualized violence. Throughout my research I learned the value of Response-Based Therapy, which I often implement when supporting clients who have experienced sexualized violence. In addition to supporting adults with mental-health challenges, I have exceptional experience working with children and youth with a range of developmental abilities. I have supported children and youth who have been diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder, Fetal Alcohol Syndrome, as well as those who struggle with Depression, Anxiety, Substance abuse, Eating disorders, self-harm, and sexual identity.
